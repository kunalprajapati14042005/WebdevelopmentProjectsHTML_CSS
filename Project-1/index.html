Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

1931 - 2015

About The Legend

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist, teacher, author and the 11th President of India. He played a crucial role in India's missile and nuclear development programs, earning the title "Missile Man of India".

His life remains an inspiration for millions of students, engineers and dreamers. He believed that knowledge, hard work and dedication could transform the future of the nation.

"Dream, Dream, Dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Major Achievements

Missile Development

Led India's missile development programs and contributed significantly to defense technology.

President of India

Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and became known as the People's President.

Pokhran-II

Played a key role in India's nuclear tests, helping strengthen national security.

Life Journey

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1960: Joined DRDO as a scientist.
  • 1969: Joined ISRO and worked on SLV projects.
  • 1998: Played a major role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002: Became the 11th President of India.
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture.
